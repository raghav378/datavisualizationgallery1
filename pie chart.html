Pie Chart

What is a Pie Chart?
A pie chart is a circular statistical graphic that is divided into slices to illustrate numerical proportions.
Each slice represents a category’s contribution to the whole, and the size of each slice is proportional to its percentage.
Pie charts are effective for showing relative proportions of a whole.

Key Characteristics of Pie Charts:
• Pie charts display parts of a whole as slices of a circle.
• The size of each slice corresponds to the proportion of the total value.
• They are best used for showing how parts compare to the whole.

Components of a Pie Chart:
• Circle – Represents the whole.
• Slices – Each slice represents a category’s contribution.
• Labels – Identify each category and its percentage.
• Legend – Provides additional information about each slice.

Types of Pie Charts:
1. Standard Pie Chart:
The classic pie chart with a single circle and slices representing categories.
2. Exploded Pie Chart:
A variation where one or more slices are separated from the rest for emphasis.
3. Donut Chart:
A variation of the pie chart with a hole in the center, often used to display percentages.

Properties of Pie Charts:
• The total of all slices should equal 100%.
• Each slice’s angle should correspond to the proportion of the whole.
• Labels or percentages should be displayed for clarity.

Uses of Pie Charts:
Pie charts are ideal for showing proportions, percentages, or parts of a whole.
They are useful when you want to emphasize the relative size of categories within a set.

Real-Life Applications:
• Showing the market share of different companies.
• Displaying the distribution of a budget.
• Visualizing survey results in categories.

Advantages & Disadvantages of Pie Charts:
Advantages:
• Easy to understand at a glance.
• Ideal for showing percentage distribution.
• Visually appealing.
Disadvantages:
• Not effective for comparing many categories.
• Can be misleading if there are too many small slices.

Drawing a Pie Chart – Step by Step:
Example data: Red: 45, Green: 20, Blue: 67, Yellow: 65, Orange: 35.

1. Choose a Title – e.g., "Favorite Colors of Students."

2. Draw a Circle – This represents the whole dataset.

3. Calculate Percentages – Determine the percentage for each category.

4. Divide the Circle – Divide the circle into slices according to the percentages.

5. Label the Slices – Add labels and percentages to each slice.
